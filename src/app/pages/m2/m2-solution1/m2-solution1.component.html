Solution

Xingming reviewed body condition, Days in Milk (DIM), and the lactating curve of the herd. Seeing low MUN, they needed to increase the RDP to get enough nitrogen in the rumen for bacteria to grow and work properly.

To address these issues, they decided to feed low producing cows the high group diet without by-passing fat.

Results

    Changing the diet increased the NEL DAIKG and Adjusted Protein.
  • After changing the diet, the MUN increased from 10.2 mg/dL to 11.5 mg/dL.
  • The milk production increased from 38.6 kg/cow/day to 40.2 kg/cow/day.
  • And the milk fat increased from 3.4% to 3.6%.
